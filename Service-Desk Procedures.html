Service Desk Procedures

Incident Management

  • Logging Incidents
    • Record details of incidents related to IT systems, medical devices, or other hospital infrastructure.
    • Capture essential information such as the nature of the incident, affected departments, and urgency.
  • Categorization and Prioritization
    • Categorize incidents based on the type of issue (e.g., IT, medical equipment).
    • Prioritize based on impact on patient care and operational urgency.
  • Incident Resolution
    • Diagnose and address the incident, coordinating with relevant medical or technical teams as needed.
    • Ensure that critical incidents affecting patient care are expedited.
  • Incident Closure
    • Confirm with the affected department or user that the issue is resolved.
    • Document the resolution and close the incident ticket in the system.

Service Request Management

  • Request Logging
    • Record service requests related to IT needs, medical equipment, or facility services.
    • Include details such as request type, urgency, and requester information.
  • Approval and Fulfillment
    • Obtain necessary approvals for service requests, especially those affecting patient care or operational efficiency.
    • Fulfill requests promptly, ensuring minimal disruption to hospital operations.
  • Request Closure
    • Verify with the requester that the service request has been fulfilled to their satisfaction.
    • Close the request ticket and document the outcome.

Problem Management

  • Problem Identification
    • Identify recurring issues or patterns that may indicate underlying problems with systems or equipment.
  • Root Cause Analysis
    • Conduct thorough investigations to determine the root cause of recurring incidents or problems.
  • Problem Resolution
    • Develop and implement solutions to address the root cause, preventing future occurrences.
    • Coordinate with relevant departments to ensure solutions are effective.
  • Problem Closure
    • Verify with stakeholders that the problem is resolved and the solution is working as intended.
    • Close the problem ticket and update the knowledge base with findings.

Change Management

  • Change Request Submission
    • Submit formal change requests for modifications to IT systems, medical equipment, or infrastructure.
    • Detail the proposed changes, benefits, risks, and impact on hospital operations.
  • Change Approval
    • Obtain approval from relevant stakeholders, including clinical and technical teams.
  • Change Implementation
    • Implement changes according to a structured plan, ensuring minimal impact on patient care and hospital operations.
  • Change Review and Closure
    • Review the effectiveness of the change and confirm successful implementation.
    • Close the change request and document outcomes.

Knowledge Management

  • Knowledge Base Maintenance
    • Maintain an up-to-date knowledge base with information on common IT and medical equipment issues, solutions, and best practices.
  • Documentation of Resolutions
    • Document resolutions for incidents and problems to facilitate quick reference and future troubleshooting.

User Communication

  • Status Updates
    • Provide regular updates to hospital staff on the status of their incidents or service requests.
  • Incident Notifications
    • Notify staff of major incidents affecting multiple departments or critical systems.
  • Feedback Collection
    • Collect feedback from users after incidents or requests are resolved to assess satisfaction and identify improvement areas.

Escalation Procedures

  • Incident Escalation
    • Escalate unresolved or critical incidents to specialized support teams or higher management.
  • Communication of Escalations
    • Inform affected departments and staff about escalations and expected resolution timelines.

Service Level Management

  • Monitoring SLAs
    • Monitor compliance with Service Level Agreements (SLAs) related to response and resolution times for incidents and requests.
  • Reporting and Reviews
    • Review and report on performance metrics, including response times, resolution rates, and user satisfaction.
