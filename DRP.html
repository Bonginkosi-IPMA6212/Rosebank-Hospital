Purpose

  • Ensure Swift Recovery: Facilitate rapid restoration of Rosebank Hospital’s critical functions during and after a disaster.

  • Protect Patient Data: Safeguard patient information and ensure its recovery.

  • Recover IT Systems: Restore essential IT systems and infrastructure to maintain hospital operations.

  • Maintain Medical Operations: Ensure continuity of medical services and patient care throughout the recovery process.

Scope

Coverage Includes

  • I.T Infrastructure

  • Electronic Health Record (EHR)
  • Communication Systems
  • Medical Devices
  • Patient Care Services

  • Physical Infrastructure

  • Power Supply
  • Water Systems
  • Medical Equipment
  • Facility Repairs & Maintenance


Risk Assessment and Impact Analysis

Risks

    Natural Disasters: Earthquakes and floods

    Cyber Threats: Ransomware, Malware Infection and data breaches

    Utility Failures: Water supply disruption & Power outages

    Internal Disasters: Fire, hazardous chemical spills

Impact

    Patient Care: Disruption of critical care services and delay in important medical procedures

    Data Integrity: Loss of patient records, breach of sensitive/confidential information

    Operational Downtime: Inability to perform surgeries, diagnostic tests, or attend to other emergency services

    Financial Impact: Increased costs due to downtime, loss of revenue, potential legal liabilities

    Public Image : Damage to the hospital's reputation if services are compromised

Preventative Measures

I.T Infrastructure

  • Conduct regular backups of the critical data to our backup server and offsite location.
  • Implementation of advanced cybersecurity measures, including IDS, firewalls, encryption, and multi-factor authentication.
  • Conduct routine updates and maintenance of all our IT systems to ensure they are up to date with the latest security updates/packages.

Physical Infrastructure

  • Installation of backup generators to ensure continuous power supply.
  • Securing medical equipment and critical infrastructure against potential damage from natural disasters.
  • Regular maintenance of plumbing and electrical systems to prevent unexpected failures.

Training & Drills

Regular Emergency Response Drills

Conduct frequent disaster recovery simulations, such as fire evacuation, system failures, or cybersecurity breaches, to ensure that personnel are well-versed in emergency protocols. simulations should be done at least quarterly to maintain preparation.

I.T Disaster Recovery Training

The hospital's technical team must recieve specialist IT training with an emphasis on data restoration techniques, system backups, and making sure that vital medical systems, such as the Electronic Health Record (EHR) system, can be quickly restored following a disaster.

Cyber Awareness Training

To make sure that every department is aware of its precise duties in the event of a crisis, create training programs that are suited to the various tasks within the hospital (such as clinical staff, the IT team, and administrative people).

Table-Top Exercises

Staff members can study the DRP in a relaxed setting by doing tabletop activities that mimic various crisis situations. This eliminates the requirement for extensive drills and guarantees that staff members comprehend recovery procedures while also assisting in identifying plan deficiencies.

Communication Strategy

Internal Communication

  • Send an emergency alert via email, SMS, and overhead announcements to all hospital staff.
  • Regular updates via email and internal communication channels every hour or as needed.
  • Department leads to hold hourly briefings with their teams to ensure all staff are informed and prepared.

External Communication

  • Utilize the hospital’s automated notification system to inform patients and their families about the situation and any necessary changes to care plans.
  • Issue press releases and updates through the hospital’s website, social media, and local media outlets.
  • Communicate with partner hospitals, suppliers, and emergency services to coordinate efforts and resources.
