Acceptable Use Policy

Purpose

  • Outline the acceptable use of the hospital’s information technology (IT) resources.
  • Protect the hospital’s patients, staff, and operations.
  • Ensure IT resources are used securely, ethically, and lawfully.

Scope

  • Applies to all employees, contractors, volunteers, hospital practitioners, and others with access to the hospital’s IT resources.
  • Covers both on-site and remote access to IT resources.

Acceptable Use

  • Use IT resources to support the hospital’s mission of providing quality patient care.
  • Access patient records, clinical applications, and hospital systems as required for job duties.
  • Use email and messaging systems for hospital-related communications.
  • Access the internet for work-related research, education, and professional development.
  • Support patient care, hospital administration, and operational efficiency through appropriate IT resource use.

Unacceptable Use

  • Accessing, transmitting, or storing illegal, offensive, discriminatory, or harassing material.
  • Using IT resources for personal gain, political activities, or unauthorized commercial purposes.
  • Accessing or sharing patient information without proper authorization, in violation of HIPAA or other privacy laws.
  • Installing or using unauthorized software, applications, or hardware that could compromise network security.
  • Downloading or distributing copyrighted materials without proper authorization.
  • Engaging in activities that could harm the hospital’s reputation or disrupt operations, such as spreading viruses or phishing.

User Responsibilities

  • Protect login credentials and avoid sharing them with others.
  • Log off or lock the computer when leaving it unattended.
  • Report suspicious activities, security incidents, or potential breaches to the IT department immediately.
  • Ensure that IT resource use complies with all applicable laws, regulations, and hospital policies.

Security Measures

  • Use only hospital-approved devices and software to access hospital systems.
  • Follow hospital protocols for the use of mobile devices, including encryption and secure connections.
  • Avoid opening email attachments or clicking on links from unknown or suspicious sources.
  • Regularly update passwords and follow hospital guidelines for password complexity.

Monitoring and Enforcement

  • The hospital reserves the right to monitor and record the use of its IT resources, including email and internet access.
  • Users should have no expectation of privacy when using hospital IT resources.
  • Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary actions, including termination, and may lead to legal action.

Compliance with Legal and Regulatory Requirements

  • Users must comply with all relevant laws and regulations, including HIPAA and other applicable national, provincial and local laws.
  • Protect patient information and ensure data security at all times.

Consequences of Violations

  • Disciplinary action, including suspension or termination of access to hospital IT resources.
  • Legal action if the violation involves illegal activities or breaches of regulatory requirements.
  • Financial penalties or damages if the violation results in harm to the hospital’s operations or reputation.

Acknowledgment

  • All users must acknowledge that they have read, understood, and agree to comply with this Acceptable Use Policy.
  • This acknowledgment may be required in writing or electronically during onboarding or periodic policy reviews.
